Purpose

Compass

At one point, I started to ask the question, “why do I exist?” After a long search, I concluded there was no real way to know because there are limits on human knowledge. In place of a definitive answer, I made my aspiration to help humanity reach a point when it might one day discover its meaning.

Today, I strive to make the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals a reality. I look for ways to make the economy circular and ensure environmental sustainability. I endeavor to get everyone equal access to opportunity so that the best ideas can flourish.

Both desires push me toward a career in Product Management, where I create products to achieve those goals. I am always searching for ways to effectuate efficient systems, accessible information, and universal health.

Character

On a much smaller scale, I realized that my privileged position at a prominent private school meant I did not have many repercussions laziness. If I focused on myself and my comfort, I could go on living as healthy as ever. It was easy to let myself fall into poor habits, such as socializing rather than working.

It may sound odd, but without societal pressure, I lost my sense of how to act. Social forces shape many behaviors, and without them, I was adrift regarding the person I wanted to become.

Eventually, I found books could be my guide. A friend of mine recommended The Fountainhead by Ayn Rand. She thought I reminded her of the protagonist, Howard Roark. I read the book and realized that Ayn Rand was right in many regards, "there are few guideposts," but Howard Roark was one of them. He provided a much-needed role model in a time of uncertainty.

Later, I came across Plato’s Republic. It instilled values like work should always be done in service to others, doing one’s work without getting in the way of others’ is justice, and that a just life is a better one.

Elon Musk’s biography is where I discovered how to enact my values in a modern context. He took his desire to prolong human existence and created a viable business based on sustainability.

Many other books shape my personality, and I regularly find more. Lab Girl and Hillbilly Elegy present useful examples of how to gracefully shoulder stress. (If you’re curious, War & Peace, Letters from a Stoic, and The Inheritance Cycle are good as well.)

For more about me as an individual, you can look at some of my role models.

Core
  • Plato's Republic - How to live a good life.

Tertiary
  • War & Peace is excellent for learning how to behave in groups.
  • Letters from a Stoic for the small things.
  • The Inheritance Cycle has beautifully illustrated stoic characters.
  • Diary from a Russian Gulag keeps the picture of what humans can endure in perspective.
  • Letters to a Young Poet - not convincing, but a good reaffirmation if you already agree with the advice.
  • Lab Girl because of how to face poverty
  • Hillbilly Elegy because of how to be responsible

How this relates to my teams

These authors helped me contemplate and think through some big questions. I hope to bring the traits exhibited by the characters in these books to the people in my life and on my product teams.

Articulating my purpose has also enabled me to more readily communicate the vision of each of my companies, which helps to inspire and motivate my teams.
